I tend to view videogames as an art still in need to be exploited experimentally to find
new avenues in human-computer interaction to incite specific sensations. Also, I tinker a lot
with human diseases such as Alzheimers, which I think is one of the most severe ones since
it alters completely our whole conception of "self".

Because of this, and with economic help from a grant I was awarded from the PECDA state program,
I created a game using Godot which tries to _confuse_ players in similar ways someone with severe
forgetfullens might feel, which include frustration and sporadic, but short lasting bliss.
